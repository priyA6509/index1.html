    MORNING
    Morning food is called breakfast in English. It is the first meal of the day, eaten in the early morning to break the overnight fasting period. Key Details About Breakfast: Purpose: It replenishes glucose levels to boost energy and alertness. Timing: Usually consumed shortly after waking up. Components: Commonly includes items like eggs, toast, oatmeal, cereal, fruit, coffee, or tea. Variations: Sometimes called "brunch" if eaten later in the morning as a combination of breakfast and lunch.
    AFTERNOON
    Afternoon food in English can refer to lunch (midday meal, e.g., sandwiches, soup) or, more specifically in British English, Afternoon Tea, a light meal (3-5 PM) with tea, sandwiches, scones, and cakes, bridging the gap to dinner. A quick snack (biscuits, fruit, yogurt) is also common, sometimes called "elevenses" (around 11 AM) or just a "break".
    EVENING
    The most common English terms for the evening meal are dinner (the main meal, often formal) and supper (a lighter, more informal evening meal or a late snack), with regional variations like tea also used in the UK. While "dinner" usually means the large evening meal in modern usage, "supper" historically referred to a midday meal when "dinner" was later, but now often means a light, casual meal at home.
    NIGHT
    The main meal eaten at night is typically called dinner (often the largest meal) or supper (usually more informal or lighter). For food consumed very late, terms like "late-night snack" or "midnight snack" are used. Regional differences exist, with "supper" sometimes referring to a smaller meal after dinner.
